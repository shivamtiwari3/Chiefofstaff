## Voice & Writing Style

<!-- Describe how you write. Be specific. -->

### Rules

- **All lowercase** -- signals person, not brand
- **Fragments that breathe** -- short beats, no connective tissue
- **Specific numbers** -- 74.5% not "about 75%" -- feels researched
- **Rhetorical pivots** -- "but the wild part?" / "now look —" -- thinking out loud
- **Add the caveat** -- builds trust, separates from clickbait
- **CTA is a direction, not a question** -- "go find your category" not "what do you think?"
- **No transitions** -- no "furthermore", "in conclusion", "I'm excited to share"
- **No em dashes** -- use short punchy lines instead

### Format Rules

- Hook must be 1-2 lines. It carries the whole post.
- No "I'm excited to share..." or "Today I want to talk about..."
- No emojis in body text
- Max 150-200 words for most posts
- Hashtags at the end: 3-5 max, relevant to your niche

## Viral Hook Formulas

Patterns from high-performing posts. Always plug in real specifics.

### 1. The Silent Shift
> "X just happened. nobody noticed."

Works because: FOMO + positions reader as insider.

### 2. The Paradox
> "[good metric]. [bad metric]. here's the trick."

Works because: cognitive dissonance forces people to keep reading.

### 3. The Specific Number
> "X% of [group] just became [surprising thing]."

Works because: specificity signals research. makes it personal to the reader.

### 4. The Personal Experiment
> "I [did unusual thing]. here's what I found."

Works because: curiosity gap + you have first-hand authority no one else has.

### 5. The First Explainer (GitHub angle)
> "this repo just hit [X] stars overnight. here's what it actually does."

Works because: first mover on LinkedIn wins. practical value for builders.
Use when: a GitHub repo trends hard -- post within 12 hours.

### 6. The Buried Story
> "X quietly [did something big]. most won't read it."

Works because: makes reader feel like they're getting intel others missed.

### 7. The Uncomfortable Observation
> "I was [doing normal work thing]. something uncomfortable stood out."

Works because: tension + authenticity. personal but relatable.

### 8. The Simple Math
> "[big number] → [another number]. do the math."

Works because: simplifies something complex. rewards the reader instantly.
